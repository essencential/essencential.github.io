Black Box Investigation

ESUMS

In our 9th Grade New Media and Tech Class, we were challenged with figuring out what’s in the Mystery Black Box originally made by Mr. Sinusas at ESUMS. We investigated and made educated guesses on how it worked and functioned with no view or sight of it. In class, we worked on our own boxes with teammates. Some boxes being a success but others being a fail, the Mystery Black Box still being a mystery.

While I observed how the Black Box functioned, I noticed how there must’ve been two ways that the water is coming out of, but when I looked at other teams boxes, most of them only had one way that the water could come out of. There were many creative and different methods I saw while building the box with my team, but none seemed to figure out what exactly was in the black box.

Modeling our own boxes in Adobe Photoshop. and Maya helped us visualize and imagine what could possibly be in the Mystery Black Box, making it much easier for us to make conclusions. Although there were some complications while using the software, some things wouldn’t work as planned. We ran into problems with modeling and animating our boxes which limited us from properly designing our ideas. Some things didn’t extrude properly, or the software crashed unexpectedly causing us to lose some work.
